About MapCamp

The Cartography Research Group Vienna is proud to host its first MapCamp – a long weekend that brings together students and practitioners of digital cartography in an environment that supports learning from each other and working on concrete projects.

MapCamp is envisioned as a self-directed gathering of people working on their projects, giving feedback to others and improving their own skills in programming and cartography. Multiple rooms will be available for concentrated work, “show and tell” sessions, peer-to-peer mentoring and socializing.



Principles

Peer-to-peer mentoring

Practitioners of different experience will participate, so you may be able to give feedback to others and receive feedback on your projects from colleagues and experts.

Project work

Every participant should work on a concrete project (possibly in a group). At the end of the event, project progress should be presented and made available online.

Cartography by programming

State-of-the-art online maps are often created by programming them from scratch, using technologies like HTML5, D3.js or WebGL. For cartographers, these new possibiliites are as exciting as challenging, so we want to focus our attention on getting better at creating maps through programming and not (primarily) on other tools in the cartographer's repertoire of techniques.

Accesibility and diversity

MapCamp is free for participants! Although we cannot currently provide any grants for travelling or accomodation, we support participation of a wide range of diverse groups and aim to create a friendly and supportive athmosphere for everyone!
